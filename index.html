Hello!

My name is Santos G. Muñoz attended University of Central Florida, where I obtained a Bachelors of Arts Digital Media in Web Design to further enhance my personal skills and knowledge. Having transferred from Broward College with Associate in Applied Science, Digital Media/Multimedia Technology. I have always had and interest for the arts no matter what type of art from; painting, drawing, photography, digital, and much more. Art runs in my family both my mother and father are very artistic, id say that is where it all started. Ever since I can remember I always said that when I grow up I want a job that is not boring but that I will enjoy waking up in the morning to go work. A job that would challenge me and push me to limits where I can grow even more.

Whether I am creating, designing, imagining, coming up with new projects and bringing them to life. It is an amazing feeling to see something from simple sketches or mockups to final rendition of the project, that is an awesome feeling. Graphic design easily became a strong suite of mine, using Photoshop, InDesign, Illustrator and a combination of other tools. Due to my interest for the arts I decided that I wanted to open my own graphic design company and be my own boss. Working for people who wanted projects that where different and that would stand out. Projects ranged from, logos, business cards, post cards, and websites. It was a challenge because as a result I had to wear different hats since I was working it alone, from designing, getting clients, marketing, and so much more. I learned a lot in the process but I wanted more knowledge in the web development side.

Knowing that I wanted to go back to school just did not know where it would be tried out several options and UCF turned out to be the best option to go with. I registered for their Digital Media Web Design track and got right into it. Yes it was difficult at times, but who does not like a good challenge. Learning different languages, platforms, how to proof your work once it was finished to make sure the code and the site did not have errors or was broken. The world wide web is always changing constantly and improving, therefore there is no room for getting bored. I am ready for what is up ahead.

Té Con Mi Amiga

The Té Con Mi Amiga website was put collectively using HTML5, CSS3 elements, and a combination of jQuery Plugins. However the design of website started inside of Photoshop CS6 with a variety of styles, brushes, gradients, layer blending, and fonts. It was tweaked until the overall look was achieved. The website is periodically updated to suit the customers needs.

Puerta de Bendición

The Puerta de Bendición Inc.website was created with Bootstrap, HTML5, CSS3, and a variation of jQuery Plugins. Designed in Photoshop CS6 using layers, imported images, styles, custom shapes, and an assortment of filters for the desired look of the website. The site is maintained on a weekly basis.

Pop Youth Group

The P.O.P Youth Group website was designed for the youth group of Puerta de Bendición Inc., it is a one page scrolling website. The navigation of the site is located along the right side of the screen. The overall look the site was aimed for a comic book feel, something with a young approach.
